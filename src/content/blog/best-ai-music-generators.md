AI music generation crossed a threshold in 2024. Early tools could make passable background loops. Today, Suno and Udio produce complete songs — vocals, lyrics, instruments, production — from a single text prompt. The quality is good enough to use in real projects.

That raises a practical question: which tool should you actually use? The answer depends entirely on what you're making — a full song, background music for a video, a film score, or a specific genre.

We tested the leading AI music generators and ranked them. Here's the breakdown.

## 1. Suno — Best Overall for Full Songs

**Pricing:** Free (10 songs/day) · Pro $8/mo · Premier $24/mo

Suno is the tool that made AI music generation go mainstream. Type "upbeat indie pop song about summer road trips" and in 20 seconds you have a complete track — drums, bass, guitar, lead vocals, and lyrics that fit the mood. It's genuinely fun to use.

The quality in v4 (released late 2025) is a significant step up from earlier versions. Vocals are more expressive, chord progressions are more interesting, and production has more depth. It still has occasional glitches — a word mispronounced, a chord change that doesn't quite land — but for AI music, it's remarkably good.

The free tier gives you 10 songs a day, which is generous for casual use. Pro unlocks commercial rights, longer songs, and priority generation.

**Best for:** Anyone who wants a complete song fast — content creators, indie musicians looking for demos, people who just want to hear their idea come to life.

**Limitation:** Less control than Udio. You can prompt and extend, but you can't easily edit individual elements.

## 2. Udio — Best for Nuanced Results

**Pricing:** Free (40 songs/mo) · Standard $10/mo · Pro $30/mo

Udio takes a slightly different approach to Suno — it tends to give more complex arrangements, richer harmonics, and more varied song structures. Where Suno gets you a solid track fast, Udio rewards experimentation and iteration.

The manual override options let you set BPM, key, and instrumentation hints. The "sections" feature lets you build a song part by part — intro, verse, chorus, bridge — rather than generating the whole thing at once. This gives experienced musicians more meaningful control.

Sound quality is excellent across genres. Hip-hop, jazz, metal, lo-fi — Udio handles diverse genres well. Vocals are strong, though Suno edges it slightly on lyric coherence.

**Best for:** Musicians who want to iterate and shape their output, producers looking for AI-assisted composition.

→ [See Udio details and pricing](/tools/udio-ai/)

## 3. Mubert — Best Royalty-Free Background Music

**Pricing:** Free · Ambassador $14/mo · Pro $39/mo

Mubert generates continuous AI music streams and downloadable tracks specifically designed for content use. It doesn't make vocal songs — instead it produces high-quality instrumental tracks across dozens of moods and genres that are safe to use in videos, podcasts, games, and apps.

Every Mubert Pro track comes with a licence certificate you can use as proof of rights clearance. It integrates directly with tools like Epidemic Sound alternatives and is used by over 100,000 content creators.

The "Render" feature lets you generate a custom track at an exact duration — useful when you need a 47-second background track to match a video cut.

**Best for:** YouTubers, video editors, app developers, podcasters who need licensed background music.

→ [See Mubert details and pricing](/tools/mubert/)

## 4. Soundraw — Best for Video Content Creators

**Pricing:** Creator $16.99/mo · Artist $29.99/mo

Soundraw is built specifically for video content. You select a mood, genre, and length, and it generates a track with an editable structure — you can move the drop, adjust the energy curve, and trim to any exact length directly in the browser.

Every track is unique (not sampled from existing music) and licensed for YouTube, social media, and commercial use on all paid plans. It doesn't have free tier generation, but the Creator plan is affordable for working video professionals.

**Best for:** Video editors, social media content creators, marketing teams who need custom-length tracks.

→ [See Soundraw details and pricing](/tools/soundraw/)

## 5. AIVA — Best for Cinematic and Classical Scores

**Pricing:** Free (3 downloads/mo) · Standard $15/mo · Pro $33/mo

AIVA (Artificial Intelligence Virtual Artist) was one of the first AI music composers and remains the strongest for orchestral, cinematic, and classical composition. It was actually the first AI to be officially recognized as a composer by a music rights society (SACEM in Luxembourg).

You can choose from dozens of style presets — film score, romantic, electronic, ambient — or upload a reference track for AIVA to match. The output tends to be more structured and "composed" than Suno or Udio, which makes it ideal for film, game, or documentary scoring.

**Best for:** Filmmakers, game developers, anyone needing scored cinematic music.

→ [See AIVA details and pricing](/tools/aiva/)

## 6. Beatoven.ai — Best for Mood-Based Tracks

**Pricing:** Free (15 min/mo) · Basic $10/mo · Pro $25/mo

Beatoven.ai lets you build tracks section by section, assigning a different mood to each part — calm intro, tense middle, uplifting resolution. It's designed with podcasters and video producers in mind.

The interface is timeline-based, familiar to anyone who's used a basic DAW. You drop in sections, pick moods, adjust tempo, and export. Less capable than Soundraw for pure music quality, but more intuitive for podcast chapter music or explainer video scoring.

→ [See Beatoven.ai details and pricing](/tools/beatoven/)

## 7. Boomy — Best for Beginners Who Want to Publish

**Pricing:** Free · Personal $9.99/mo · Pro $29.99/mo

Boomy is the simplest entry point into AI music. Click a genre, click generate, done. The output is basic — more template-driven than generative — but the real draw is Boomy's built-in publishing pipeline. With one click, you can distribute your AI song to Spotify, Apple Music, and TikTok and start collecting streaming royalties.

Millions of Boomy songs are on streaming platforms. It's not for quality-focused musicians, but it's perfect for hobbyists who want to experiment with releasing music.

→ [See Boomy details and pricing](/tools/boomy/)

---

## 8. Soundful — Best for Studio-Quality Stems

**Pricing:** Free (5 downloads/mo) · Creator $9.99/mo · Producer $49.99/mo

Soundful focuses on production quality and stem export. Stems are the individual track components — drums, bass, melody — which you can import into a DAW and edit like a real session. For music producers who want AI as a starting point rather than a finished product, Soundful is the most production-ready option.

→ [See Soundful details and pricing](/tools/soundful/)

## How to Choose

**You want a complete song with vocals:** Suno or Udio

**You need background music for videos:** Mubert or Soundraw

**You're scoring a film or game:** AIVA

**You want to release music to streaming platforms:** Boomy

**You want stems to edit in a DAW:** Soundful

**You're a podcaster:** Beatoven.ai
