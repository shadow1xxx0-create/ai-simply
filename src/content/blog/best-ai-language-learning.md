## 1. Duolingo — Best for Beginners

**Pricing:** Free · Duolingo Super $6.99/mo · Max $13.99/mo

Duolingo is the most downloaded education app in the world for good reason. Its gamification — streaks, XP, leagues, animated characters — makes daily practice genuinely enjoyable. For beginners who struggle to build a consistent habit, Duolingo's system is unmatched.

The AI now powers personalized review, conversation practice with an AI tutor (Max plan), and adaptive difficulty. The lessons are short (3-5 minutes), making them easy to fit in anywhere.

For popular languages (Spanish, French, German, Japanese), the courses are deep and well-structured. For rarer languages, quality varies.

**Limitation:** Heavy gamification can feel shallow. Advanced learners often find it too easy and not focused enough on production.

**Best for:** Absolute beginners, people who struggle with consistency, anyone who wants to try language learning for free.

## 2. Babbel — Best for Conversational Skills

**Pricing:** Monthly $14.95 · Annual $83.40 ($6.95/mo)

Babbel is designed by linguists with one goal: get you speaking real conversations as fast as possible. Lessons are scenario-based ("at the airport," "meeting a colleague," "ordering food") and use AI speech recognition to correct your pronunciation immediately.

The structured curriculum is more rigorous than Duolingo — lessons build on each other in a logical sequence rather than scattering topics across levels. Most learners reach confident A2-B1 conversational ability within 3-6 months of consistent use.

14 languages only — but all are done well. No filler languages with thin content.

**Best for:** Motivated adults who want practical conversation skills, travelers, professionals learning for work.

→ [See Babbel details and pricing](/tools/babbel/)

---

## 3. Pimsleur — Best for Speaking Confidence

**Pricing:** Premium $14.95/mo · All Access $20.95/mo

Pimsleur's method is unique: you never look at text. Lessons are 30-minute audio programs built on a spaced repetition system — a voice asks you a question in English, waits, then gives you the answer in the target language. You speak out loud, and the AI adjusts what you hear based on your confidence ratings.

The approach produces remarkably fast speaking ability. After 30 lessons (one month), most learners can hold basic conversations. The audio-first method also means you develop an ear for the language, not just reading skills.

**Limitation:** Expensive per language. No writing practice. Vocabulary breadth is narrower than Babbel or Duolingo.

**Best for:** Commuters, audio learners, people who want to speak confidently before they can read well.
